Cinéville

A conceptual poster design for an international film festival, blending 1920s Surrealism with a modern, eclectic aesthetic to capture the creative audacity of independent cinema.

Role: Graphic Designer, Art Direction
Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator
Year: 2024

The Challenge

The mission was to design a poster for Cinéville, an annual festival celebrating independent and international films. The poster needed to capture the festival's unique spirit—eclectic, accessible, and combining chic with a relaxed atmosphere—while reflecting the energy and creative audacity of independent filmmaking.

Artistic Direction

The core concept was rooted in the visual language of 1920-1930s Surrealism, creating a dreamlike and thought-provoking aesthetic. The design leverages key surrealist codes like dream imagery, juxtaposition, and symbolism to represent profound ideas and emotions.

The central element, a melting film reel, is a direct reference to Salvador Dalí's "The Persistence of Memory." In this context, it symbolizes the audacity of international cinema and its power to challenge conventional perceptions of time and reality. This is complemented by clouds inspired by René Magritte, representing imagination, and cinema seats under a starry sky, evoking a magical and universally accessible space.
